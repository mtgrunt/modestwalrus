## About This Song
"The Light" was released in 2000 on Common's album *Like Water for Chocolate*, produced by James Poyser and built around a sample of Bobby Caldwell's "Open Your Eyes." Part of the Soulquarians-era work that defined the album's sound, it became one of Common's biggest and most accessible singles; a warm, melodic love song that stood apart from the more conscious, abstract material he was known for at the time.

## First Impressions
The sample loop is gorgeous from the first bar, warm and inviting in a way that immediately sets it apart from a typical rap beat. Common's flow sits right in the pocket, conversational and sincere.

## My Reaction
"The Light" works because Common sounds completely at ease in it. The lyricism is direct without losing any of the wit and wordplay he's known for, and the live setting only sharpened how genuine it all feels. It's one of those rare hip-hop love songs that doesn't oversell the romance; it just lets the warmth of the music carry it.
