## Why I Reacted To This Song
Thelonious Monk is one of the great jazz pianists. His approach to the instrument is unlike anyone else; angular, unpredictable and completely his own. "'Round Midnight" is the song that defines him and it demanded a reaction.

## About This Song
"'Round Midnight" was written by Thelonious Monk around 1944 and has since become one of the most recorded jazz standards in history. The title captures the mood perfectly; a piece that sounds like the loneliness and stillness of the late-night hours. Monk's harmonic language was ahead of its time, full of dissonance and surprise, and nowhere is that more apparent than here. Miles Davis, Coltrane and countless others have covered it, but Monk's own recordings remain the definitive versions.

## First Impressions
From the very first notes the mood is set. There is a heaviness to it, a weight that pulls you in immediately. Monk's touch on the keys is like nothing else; he leaves space where other pianists fill it and fills space where others leave it. It takes a moment to settle into his rhythm but once you do the whole thing opens up.

## My Reaction
"'Round Midnight" is a masterpiece of atmosphere and restraint. Monk plays with a personality so distinct you could identify him in two bars. The harmonic choices are bold and the melody has a haunting quality that stays with you long after it ends. This is jazz at its most expressive; not flashy, not busy, just deeply felt. A great jazz pianist at the peak of his craft.
