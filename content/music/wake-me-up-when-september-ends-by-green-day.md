## Why I Reacted To This Song
I was in middle school when I heard Green Day for the first time. Evolving to recent days, Green Day was my jam. Father's Day just passed and I'm missing my father, so this one hit differently this time around.

## About This Song
"Wake Me Up When September Ends" was written by Billie Joe Armstrong and released in 2004 on Green Day's album *American Idiot*. Armstrong wrote it about losing his own father to cancer when he was just 10 years old, and that grief runs through every line. It became one of the band's biggest singles and, despite later being associated with other tragedies because of its timing, remains at its core a deeply personal song about loss.

If this song hits home for you the way it does for me, consider a donation to the [American Cancer Society](https://www.cancer.org).

## First Impressions
The acoustic intro is instantly recognisable, and it carries a weight before a single lyric even lands. It's one of those songs that feels heavier the more life you've lived alongside it.

## My Reaction
"Wake Me Up When September Ends" holds up because it never tries to be bigger than the grief it's actually about. Armstrong's vocal is restrained where it could've been overwrought, and that restraint is what makes it land so hard. Listening to it now, missing my own father, it means more than it did back in middle school. Some songs grow with you.
