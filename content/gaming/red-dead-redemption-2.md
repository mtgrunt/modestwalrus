## My Reaction
Red Dead Redemption 2 is widely regarded as one of the greatest games of all time, and it's easy to see why. The graphics, the storytelling and the detailed open world all come together into something that feels like a genuine artistic achievement, even if the pacing and some of the gameplay mechanics can be frustrating at times.

## Overview
Red Dead Redemption 2 follows Arthur Morgan and the Van der Linde gang through a sprawling, expansive open world exploring themes of loyalty and survival. The world is filled with diverse activities, characters and side missions, and the gameplay combines action, exploration and character interactions across a slow-burn narrative.

## What I Liked
- Stunning visuals with incredible attention to detail, particularly in landscapes and environments.
- An engaging narrative that follows Arthur Morgan and the gang through themes of loyalty and survival.
- Character development is exceptional; players connect deeply with the cast, which enhances the emotional impact of the story.
- The artistic quality and depth make it a significant talking point among gamers, and a genuine masterpiece for many.
- An expansive world filled with activities and side content that rewards exploration.

## What I Didn't Like
- Certain gameplay mechanics, such as the auto-aim feature, can detract from the experience.

## Verdict
Red Dead Redemption 2 earns its reputation as a masterpiece through sheer artistic ambition; the world, the characters and the story all land. The slow pacing and some clunky mechanics hold it back from perfection, but the overall experience is unforgettable.

86/100
