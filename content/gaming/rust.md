## My Reaction
Rust is one of the most brutal PVP survival games out there. Anyone who plays or has just started playing Rust knows that this is a game with many ups and downs. At the beginning you are an ordinary "naked" who spawned with a rock and a torch by the beach, and in the next moment you already have your base and weapons. It depends on you and your skill, whether you become an unstoppable force on the server, or you just return naked to the beach.

## Overview
Rust drops you into a harsh, open-world survival sandbox where the only guarantee is uncertainty. You scavenge, craft, build and defend a base while other players do exactly the same, often at your expense. There's one rule in Rust: if you killed one, there's a high probability he wasn't wandering alone.

## What I Liked
- The progression from naked beach spawn to a fortified base and full kit is genuinely satisfying.
- Playing with one or more friends is one of the most important things about this game; you progress faster, watch each other's backs and generally enjoy the gameplay far more.
- The constant tension of PVP encounters keeps every session unpredictable.
- Base building and raiding add real strategic depth beyond just gunfights.

## What I Didn't Like
- Server wipes mean progress is never permanent, which won't appeal to everyone.

## Verdict
Rust earns its reputation as one of the toughest survival games around. It's at its best with a squad; recommend it to your friends if they haven't picked it up already, because going in alone is a very different and much harsher experience.
