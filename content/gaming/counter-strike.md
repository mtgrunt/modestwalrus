## My Reaction
Counter-Strike was very unique in 2000. Multiplayer gaming was still in an early stage and Counter-Strike changed the game completely. We played it in the dorms over T1, and it was unlike anything else; a tactical team-based shooter when most multiplayer shooters were still pure run-and-gun. I was into it.

## Overview
Counter-Strike began as a mod for Half-Life before Valve brought it in-house and published it as a standalone title. It pits a team of Terrorists against a team of Counter-Terrorists across objective-based rounds; planting or defusing bombs, rescuing or guarding hostages. There's no respawning mid-round, so every life matters and every engagement carries weight. It became one of the most influential multiplayer tactical shooters ever made.

## What I Liked
- The round-based, no-respawn structure made every life feel valuable in a way no other shooter at the time did.
- The economy system; buying weapons and gear round to round added a real strategic layer.
- Team coordination and communication mattered more than raw aim.
- It laid the foundation for competitive esports shooters that's still being built on today.
- The legacy is incredible; Counter-Strike 2 is still a staple two decades later.

## What I Didn't Like
- The learning curve was steep early on; new players got punished hard before they understood the economy and map callouts.

## Verdict
Counter-Strike redefined what multiplayer shooters could be. The tension of round-based tactical play, the depth of the buy system and the purity of the gunplay made it essential during a time when online multiplayer was still finding its footing. Its DNA is still alive in Counter-Strike 2 today.
