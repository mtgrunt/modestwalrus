Gaming: Halo 2

A review and reaction to Halo 2 by Bungie. A landmark first-person shooter sequel for the Xbox. Rated 92/100.

June 29, 2026 • 247 words • 2 min
first-person shooter FPS Bungie Halo 2 Xbox 2004 sci-fi classic games shooter games

Game/Publisher/Developer

Halo 2, Microsoft Game Studios, Bungie

The Trailer

My Reaction

Everything’s designed with a master’s eye for first-person intensity, extensively crafted and technically superb. The genuine care that has been taken in making this game is awe inspiring.

Overview

Halo 2 is a first-person shooter developed by Bungie and published by Microsoft Game Studios, released in 2004 for the Xbox. The direct sequel to Halo: Combat Evolved, it continues the Master Chief’s fight against the Covenant while introducing a dual storyline that also follows the Arbiter, a disgraced Covenant warrior. The game introduced dual-wielding weapons, new vehicles like the Brute Chopper, and became a defining title for Xbox Live, helping push console online multiplayer into the mainstream.

What I Liked

  • The dual-wielding weapon system adds real tactical variety to encounters.
  • Telling part of the story from the Arbiter’s perspective gives the Covenant actual depth instead of just being faceless enemies.
  • Level and encounter design shows obsessive attention to detail, every fight feels deliberately built.
  • The multiplayer suite, paired with Xbox Live, set the standard for console shooters for years afterward.
  • The sound design and score elevate every major set piece.

What I Didn’t Like

  • The cliffhanger ending leaves the campaign feeling unresolved on its own.

Verdict

Halo 2 is technically superb in a way that still holds up; every system feels considered and every encounter feels crafted rather than assembled. It’s a high point for the franchise and for console FPS design in general.

Rating

92/100
