Gaming: Warcraft II

A review and reaction to Warcraft II: Tides of Darkness by Blizzard Entertainment. A landmark real-time strategy classic. Rated 88/100.

June 9, 2026 • 396 words • 2 min
real-time strategy RTS Blizzard Entertainment Warcraft II Tides of Darkness PC classic games strategy games 1995

Game/Publisher/Developer

Warcraft II, NA: Davidson & Associates / EU: Zablac Entertainment, Blizzard Entertainment

My Reaction

Warcraft II hooked me immediately. The choice between humans and orcs felt meaningful and the moment you start building your base and training units the game just pulls you in. The strategic depth was unlike anything else at the time. This was an easy buy and one I kept coming back to.

Overview

Warcraft II: Tides of Darkness is a real-time strategy game developed by Blizzard Entertainment and released in 1995. Players choose between the Alliance (humans) and the Horde (orcs), each with unique units, buildings and abilities. The goal is to gather resources; gold, wood and oil; build a base, train an army and crush the enemy. It features a full campaign for both factions, custom scenarios and multiplayer. A landmark title that helped define the RTS genre.

What I Liked

  • The two-faction system gives the game genuine replay value; humans and orcs play differently
  • Resource management is deep but accessible; gathering gold, wood and oil never feels tedious
  • The unit variety is excellent, from basic footmen and grunts to dragons and death knights
  • The campaign storytelling is surprisingly engaging for its era
  • The naval warfare adds a dimension most RTS games of the time didn’t attempt
  • Memorable unit dialogue, clicking units repeatedly rewards you with some of the best humor in gaming

What I Didn’t Like

  • Pathfinding can be frustrating, units don’t always take the most sensible routes
  • Managing large armies gets clunky without modern quality-of-life controls

Verdict

Warcraft II is a classic for good reason. The strategic depth, faction variety and sheer fun of building and commanding an army have never gotten old. Whether you played it in 1995 or discovered it through the remaster, it holds up. One of Blizzard’s finest.

Rating

88/100

Session Notes

Went straight into the orc campaign. There’s something deeply satisfying about sending a pack of grunts and a couple of trolls into an enemy base and watching it fall apart. Got distracted managing the naval units; the juggernaught is an absolute beast. Lost track of time completely.

Thoughts

Don’t neglect your navy on water maps; it can be the deciding factor. Upgrade your units early and keep your Peons and Peons busy; falling behind on resources is hard to recover from. The orc campaign is worth playing through for the story alone.
