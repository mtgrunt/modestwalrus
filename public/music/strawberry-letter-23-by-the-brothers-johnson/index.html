Reaction Strawberry Letter 23 by The Brothers Johnson

A reaction to Strawberry Letter 23 by The Brothers Johnson, a funk classic with one of the all-time great basslines. Rated 86/100.

June 25, 2026 • 207 words • 1 min
funk soul The Brothers Johnson Strawberry Letter 23 1977 Right on Time Quincy Jones bass

Why I Reacted To This Song

That was a freakin’ vibe. The bassline was unheard of, very “slap the bass,” and it demanded a reaction the moment it kicked in.

Original by Artist

About This Song

“Strawberry Letter 23” was originally written and recorded by Shuggie Otis in 1971, but it’s The Brothers Johnson’s 1977 version, produced by Quincy Jones for their album Right on Time, that became the definitive take. Louis Johnson’s bass playing turned the song into a showcase for the slap-bass technique he helped popularize, and it became the duo’s biggest hit, topping the Billboard Soul singles chart.

First Impressions

The bassline hits and everything else becomes secondary for a second. It’s psychedelic, funky and impossibly smooth all at once, and it sets a tone the rest of the track never lets up on.

My Reaction

“Strawberry Letter 23” is a masterclass in groove. Louis Johnson’s slap bass is unlike anything else from the era; it’s melodic and rhythmic at the same time, practically a lead instrument in its own right. The production from Quincy Jones gives everything room to breathe while still sounding lush. This is one of those tracks where the bassline alone earns it a place in the canon.

Rating

86/100
