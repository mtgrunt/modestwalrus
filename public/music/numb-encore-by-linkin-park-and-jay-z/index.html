Reaction Numb/Encore by Linkin Park & JAY-Z

A reaction to Numb/Encore, the genre-bridging Linkin Park and JAY-Z collaboration from Collision Course. Rated 85/100.

July 15, 2026 • 200 words • 1 min
rap rock mashup Linkin Park JAY-Z Numb/Encore Collision Course 2004 genre collaboration live performance

Why I Reacted To This Song

Two acts that had no business sounding this good together somehow made it work, and the live performance takes it even further.

Original by Artist

About This Song

“Numb/Encore” arrived in 2004 as a genuinely unexpected pairing, a joint effort between JAY-Z and Linkin Park that blends JAY-Z’s sharp rap verses with Linkin Park’s atmospheric rock sound. The track came out of the Collision Course EP, a project built specifically to close the gap between two genres that rarely shared the same stage, mashing “Numb” and “Encore” into a single song rather than just trading verses over one beat.

First Impressions

The way “Numb” and “Encore” lock together feels almost inevitable once you hear it, like the two songs were always meant to occupy the same space.

My Reaction

Even nearly two decades on, this one still holds up as a meaningful piece of music history. Its success opened the door for other cross-genre collaborations and showed that music really could bring very different audiences together. With genre lines blurring more every year, this pairing still stands as a reminder of what innovation and a willingness to take unexpected paths can produce.

Rating

85/100
